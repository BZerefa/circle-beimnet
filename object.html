Image

Although digital objects can be simple or complex, I chose to focus on a simple digital object made up of a single file; an image. In this short essay, I aim to conduct a formal analysis of both the metadata and informational content of an image I took. The digital object I have chosen is a photo of a necklace that appears to be composed of both metallic and brightly colored beadwork.

Exif Data

Using the exif-test code I wrote in class, I got the metadata of this image and will be approaching it from an anti-interpretive digital humanities perspective to start. According to the authors of “Neoliberal Tools (and Archives): A Political History of Digital Humanities,” “Digital Humanities instead aims to archive materials, produce data, and develop software, while bracketing off the work of interpretation to a later moment or leaving it to other scholars…” (Allington et al 2016).
