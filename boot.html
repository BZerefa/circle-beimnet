To paraphrase, he carefully considered the practical setup and social interactions within the laboratory, viewing it not only as a technological venture but also as a social experiment. The lab’s layout was unique for its time: while each person had a private office, the actual work took place in a shared "bullpen" area with movable terminals and open space. Engelbart believed this open layout encouraged communication, organizing the environment according to his idea of "social ergonomics." I think this was the key that points to the connection between tech and social interaction not just after innovation but before, and as a catalyst for it as well.
